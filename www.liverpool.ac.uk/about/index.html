About us sections

About us

For advancement of learning and ennoblement of life

This has been our mission since the University was founded in 1881. To this day, these values focus our efforts as we strive to achieve our ambitions and tackle the grand challenges of the age.

  • Our response to the Equality and Human Rights Commission report on tackling racial harassment.

An inspirational centre of learning

Our aim is to support our students as they become highly employable, creative, and culturally rich graduates, with the capacity to find employment that will enable them to be agents for change in a connected world.

World-class teaching and learning

  • Research-connected curricula
  • Teaching delivered by leading experts
  • 22,000+ students on campus
  • Global exchange opportunities
  • £800 million investment in our teaching, research and residential estate
  • 400+ Undergraduate and Postgraduate courses.

Strong international student community

  • 7,700+ international students on campus
  • 10,000+ online students from over 160 countries
  • Network of 219,000 alumni in 171 countries
  • Dedicated international support team.

Institution-wide commitment to widening participation

  • Institution-wide commitment to widening participation
  • Providing opportunities for young people and local schools
  • Significant investment in bursaries and scholarships
  • Contextual admissions for applicants who experience barriers
  • Foundation programmes to support transition in to Year 1
  • Hundreds of undergraduates employed as role models.

The Liverpool advantage

Associated with nine Nobel Laureates, the University is recognised for its high-quality teaching and research. Our research collaborations extend worldwide and address many of the grand challenges facing mankind today.

Research powerhouse

  • Collaborations with 1,300 external organisations
  • An internationally renowned Russell Group university
  • Associated with 9 Nobel Prize winners
  • 91% of research is world leading or internationally excellent.

Leading progress

  • Personalised health
  • Advanced materials
  • Infectious diseases
  • Heritage
  • Digital
  • Starting well, living well, ageing well
  • Climate futures

Spanning all faculties

  • Health & Life Sciences
  • Science & Engineering
  • Humanities & Social Sciences.

Sharing expertise

  • 14 Doctoral Training Centres
  • 100+ Postgraduate Research opportunities
  • Major research networks
  • Continuous professional development programmes
  • Strong links with industry, community and government
  • Dedicated business and consultancy services.

A thrilling world city 

The University is driving the city’s knowledge economy through close collaboration with fellow universities, industry and the NHS, helping the Liverpool City Region compete in the global business world.

 

Heart and soul

  • 445,000 population
  • 80 languages spoken at home
  • UK's oldest Chinese community
  • Six in ten graduates remain in the city

Knowledge economy

  • Three universities
  • Home of 50,000+ students
  • 36,000+ active businesses
  • Projected 15% growth in knowledge economy by 2022

Arts and culture

  • Thriving arts, music and cultural capital including our own concert hall - The Tung Auditorium
  • 54 million visitors annually
  • World Heritage skyline
  • Birthplace of the Beatles
  • Two Premier League football clubs
  • Most museums and galleries outside London including our own Victoria Gallery & Museum.

On every continent 

The University is a globally-focused institution with partnerships throughout the world, including research institutes, universities, governments and industrial organisations.

Connected University

  • Ground-breaking Year in China programme
  • 7,000+ international students in Liverpool
  • Undergraduate exchange partners around the world
  • Cross-sectoral international connections
  • Languages at the centre of our strategy.

Our campus locations

  • Liverpool city centre
  • Leahurst (Wirral)
  • Suzhou, China

Online learning

  • The UK's leading international provider of fully online postgraduate education.
  • 100% online programmes
  • Collaborative, supported learning.

Our sustainable University 

The University is working in partnerships both locally and globally to address the UN Sustainable Development Goals, harnessing our strengths in research and education to drive forward the solutions and cultural change needed to move to a sustainable future.

Tackling climate change

  • Discover how our research is helping to address the climate crisis
  • Exploring the impact of climate change on society
  • Developing solutions to address climate change
  • Achieving net-zero carbon by 2035

Sustainability in practice

  • Smarter buildings and beekeeping creating a sustainable Liverpool campus
  • Hedgerows and woodlands support plants and animals at Leahurst campus
  • Numerous plants and ponds maintain biodiversity at Ness Botanic Gardens.

Greener campus

  • £19 million invested in heating and infrastructure
  • 90% of our electricity is generated on campus
  • Minimising our carbon footprint at Greenbank student village
  • Hedgehog-friendly campus gold award.

"Our vision is to be a connected, global University at the forefront of knowledge leadership."

- Vice-Chancellor,  Professor Dame Janet Beer
