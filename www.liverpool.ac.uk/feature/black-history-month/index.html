Black History Month

A celebration of Black history, arts and culture throughout the UK. Our Racial Equity Challenge will encourage you to build and act on anti-racist principles and skills, find out more about exhibitions in the city and learn about important figures sidelined from musical history.

Racial equity challenge

Racial Equity Challenge

Our Diversity and Equality Team have created a month-long Racial Equity Challenge. For each working day in October, there will be a resource or activity to engage with, with the aim of further building and acting on anti-racist principles and skills.

Sister Rosetta Tharpe, one of many women sidelined from musical history

Dr Freya Jarman from our Department of Music writes about the black women singers and musicians who forged the way for artists like Elvis Presley.

The World Reimagined

A nationwide programme of art education around Black history and achievements. In Liverpool, you can visit an exhibition at the Victoria Gallery and Museum or explore a sculpture trail with points of historical interest across the city centre.

Black History Month at National Museums Liverpool

The programme at Museums Liverpool includes thought-provoking displays, topical talks and discussions, a series of film screenings and storytelling for children.

Black History Month around Liverpool

Find out more about events taking place in the city, events you can join online, and more resources to learn from.

Dr Mzwandile Mabhala presents his health inequalities research

The University of Liverpool's BAME Staff Network and LSTM BAME Network Committee jointly welcome Dr Mzwandile Mabhala to present his research on some of the causes of health inequalities. Book your place here.
